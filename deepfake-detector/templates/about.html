The Project

About DeepScan

A diploma project focused on using machine learning to fight synthetic media and protect digital trust.

Why This Matters

Deepfakes — AI-generated or manipulated images — are becoming increasingly realistic and accessible. They pose serious threats to personal reputation, political integrity, and public trust in digital media.

DeepScan was built as a response to this growing problem. We implemented and compared two approaches: a CNN trained from scratch to demonstrate original work, and EfficientNet fine-tuned for comparison — showing the trade-off between originality and performance.

"Seeing is no longer believing — but AI can help us find the truth."

Scope & Limitations

DeepScan is designed specifically for image-based deepfake detection. The current version does not support:

  • Video deepfake detection
  • Audio manipulation detection
  • Real-time webcam analysis

These may be explored in future versions of the project.
