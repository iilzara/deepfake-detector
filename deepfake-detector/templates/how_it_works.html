The Technology

How It Works

A deep dive into both model architectures, training process, and detection pipeline.

01

Image Upload

You upload a portrait image through the web interface. Accepted in JPG, PNG, or WEBP format under 16MB.

02

Preprocessing

The image is resized to 224×224 pixels, normalized across RGB channels, and converted to a PyTorch tensor — identical to training.

03

Model Inference

The tensor passes through either our custom CNN or EfficientNet-B0. Both extract visual features like texture artifacts and blending inconsistencies.

04

Classification

The classifier outputs two scores — REAL and FAKE. Softmax converts these into a probability, giving you a confidence percentage.

📦

Dataset

Trained on 36k images — 18k real faces and 18k AI-generated deepfakes from GAN and modern generators like Midjourney and DALL-E.

⚙️

Training Setup

Both models trained on Google Colab T4 GPU. CNN: 20 epochs, AdamW. EfficientNet: 10 epochs, Adam with step scheduler.

🔀

Data Augmentation

Random flips, rotations (±15°), color jitter, random grayscale, and random perspective to improve generalization.

🛡️

Overfitting Prevention

Dropout layers (0.1–0.5), BatchNorm after each convolution, weight decay, and label smoothing keep both models generalizing well.
